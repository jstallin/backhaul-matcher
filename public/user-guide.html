Section 1
Setting Up Your Fleet

Your fleet profile is the foundation of HaulMonitor. It stores your equipment details, home base location, and the rate structure used to calculate net revenue on every backhaul opportunity. Complete your fleet profile before creating any requests.

1
Navigate to Fleet Setup

Open the navigation menu (☰) in the top-right corner of any page and select Fleets. On the Fleets page, click Create Fleet Profile to open the setup form.

Screenshot: Navigation menu → Fleets
2
Enter Fleet Information

Fill in your fleet's basic contact and identification details.

Fleet Name *
Your company or fleet name (e.g., Carolina Transport Fleet)
MC Number
Your FMCSA Motor Carrier number (e.g., MC-123456)
DOT Number
Your USDOT number (e.g., DOT-123456)
Fleet Manager Phone *
Used for text notifications when opportunities change
Fleet Manager Email *
Used for email notifications
3
Set Your Home Base Location

Enter your fleet's home base address — this is where your trucks return to after deliveries. HaulMonitor uses this to calculate out-of-route miles for each backhaul opportunity.

Home Base Address *
City and state or full street address (e.g., Davidson, NC or 123 Fleet Dr, Davidson, NC 28036)
💡 After you type the address and tab away, HaulMonitor automatically verifies it using PC*MILER and shows a ✓ Verified confirmation with the exact coordinates. No manual lookup needed.
Screenshot: Address field with ✓ Verified status
4
Configure Rate Structure

The rate configuration section drives all financial calculations in your backhaul and estimate reports. Scroll down past the fleet information to find it.

Revenue Split
How gross load revenue is divided between your fleet (carrier) and the shipper (customer).
Carrier % Split
Percentage of load revenue paid to your fleet. The remaining % is the Customer Net Credit. Default: 20% to customer, 80% to carrier.
Mileage & Stop Rates
Rates charged for out-of-route miles and extra stops created by taking the backhaul.
Mileage Rate ($/mi)
Your cost per out-of-route mile (e.g., 2.50)
Stop Rate ($/stop)
Cost per additional stop added by the backhaul (e.g., 75.00)
Fuel Surcharge (FSC)
HaulMonitor can calculate FSC automatically using live DOE fuel price data, or you can enter a fixed rate.
Fuel Peg ($/gal)
Your baseline fuel cost per gallon used in the FSC formula
Truck MPG
Average fuel economy of your fleet (default: 6.0 mpg)
PADD Region
Select your region for live DOE diesel price feeds. HaulMonitor fetches the current average automatically.
💡 FSC is calculated as: (DOE Diesel Price − Fuel Peg) ÷ MPG per mile. When a PADD region is selected, the DOE price updates automatically.
Other Charges Optional
Up to two additional per-load charges (e.g., lumper fees, trailer tracking).
Charge Name
Short label shown on reports (e.g., Lumper Fee)
Description
Brief explanation (max 25 characters)
Amount ($/load)
Fixed cost per load
Screenshot: Rate Configuration section
5
Save the Fleet Profile

Click Save Fleet Profile at the bottom of the form. You'll see a success confirmation and be returned to the Fleets list. You can edit your fleet profile at any time — changes take effect on the next request run.

Section 2
Creating a Backhaul Request

A Backhaul Request tells HaulMonitor where your truck currently is (the datum point) and when the equipment needs to be back home. The system searches live load data, scores every opportunity by out-of-route efficiency and net revenue, and presents the best matches in a ranked list with a route map.

1
Open the Request Form

From the navigation menu (☰), select Start Backhaul Request.

Screenshot: Navigation menu → Start Backhaul Request
2
Fill in the Request Details
Request Name *
A descriptive name to identify this request (e.g., Backhaul Request 12/30/2025)
Datum Point *
Where your truck currently is or will be at the start of its return home. Enter a city/state or ZIP (e.g., Charlotte, NC or 28036).
Select Fleet *
Choose which fleet this request is for. The fleet's home base and rate config are used for all calculations.
Equipment Available *
The earliest date the truck is available to pick up a backhaul load
Equipment Needed Back *
The date the equipment must be returned home by
Screenshot: Backhaul Request form fields
3
Set Options (Relay, Auto-Refresh, Notifications)
Relay Request Optional
Enable this if the load will be handed off to a relay driver rather than your truck running the full route home.
Auto Refresh Optional
HaulMonitor can automatically re-run the search on a schedule so your results stay current without manual intervention.
Refresh Interval
How often to re-run the match (30 min, 1 hr, 2 hr, 4 hr, 8 hr, 24 hr)
💡 Even with auto-refresh off, you can always click Refresh Now on the Open Requests page to re-run the search on demand.
Notifications Optional
Get alerted when auto-refresh detects a change in the top result.
Notification Method
Email, Text (SMS), or Both — sent to the phone and email on your fleet profile
4
Submit the Request

Click Find Backhaul Opportunities. HaulMonitor will geocode the datum point, build the route corridor from your current location to fleet home, and score all matching loads. This typically takes 5–15 seconds.

5
Review Results on the Open Requests Page

After submitting, your request appears in Open Backhaul Requests. Click the request card to see the full results — a ranked list of backhaul opportunities and a route map showing opportunities along the corridor home.

Rank / Net Credit
Opportunities are ranked by Customer Net Credit (highest first). The best load shows a 🥇 badge.
Carrier Revenue
Total revenue your fleet earns from the load (gross)
OOR Miles
Out-of-route miles added versus driving straight home
Net Credit
Revenue minus mileage, stop, and fuel surcharge costs — your bottom-line benefit per load
Screenshot: Open Requests results page with route map and ranked list
⚠️ Results are estimates only. Always validate mileage figures with your own routing engine before committing to a load.
Section 3
Creating an Estimate Request

An Estimate Request is a planning tool. Instead of searching for a specific live load, it projects the annual financial value of backhaul opportunities along a given route — useful for customer proposals, pricing negotiations, and fleet planning. The output is a formatted report showing highest, top-5 average, and overall average net credit per load.

1
Open the Estimate Request Form

From the navigation menu (☰), select Create Estimate Request.

Screenshot: Navigation menu → Create Estimate Request
2
Fill in the Request Details
Request Name *
A descriptive label for this estimate (e.g., Estimate Request 03/05/2026)
Datum Point *
The starting location for the return route. Enter a city/state or ZIP (e.g., Charlotte, NC or 28036).
Select Fleet *
The fleet whose rate config will be used to calculate financials
Equipment Available *
Start of the date window to search for available loads
Equipment Needed Back *
End of the date window — equipment must be home by this date
Annual Volume
Expected number of loads per year. Used to project annual revenue totals on the report (e.g., 52 for weekly).
Minimum Net Credit
Only opportunities meeting or exceeding this net credit per load are included in averages and totals (e.g., $100). Keeps low-value or negative loads from skewing results.
Screenshot: Estimate Request form
3
Relay Mode (Optional)

Enable Relay Request if the backhaul will be run by a relay driver rather than the outbound truck. In relay mode, mileage is calculated as the relay driver's full round trip (home → pickup → delivery → home) minus the deadhead back, giving a more accurate cost picture for relay operations.

4
Submit and View the Report

Click Create Estimate Request. HaulMonitor searches for matching opportunities and generates a formatted estimate report. The report is saved under Open Estimate Requests and can be printed or saved as a PDF at any time.

Screenshot: Create Estimate Request button
5
Reading the Estimate Report

The report presents three columns — Highest Net, Average (All), and Top 5 Average — across four metric groups.

Customer Credit
Net Credit per Load
The bottom-line credit per load: carrier revenue share minus all OOR costs (miles, stops, FSC)
Annual Credit
Net Credit × Annual Volume — total projected yearly benefit
Route Activity
Carrier Miles (per load)
Out-of-route miles added per load versus driving straight home
Annual Mileage Add
Total additional miles driven across the full annual volume
Stops Added (per load)
Number of extra stops per load (pickup + delivery = 2 typical)
Carrier Annual Revenue Components
Backhaul % Split
Annual revenue from your carrier percentage of gross load revenue
OOR Miles
Annual cost of out-of-route miles (miles × mileage rate × volume)
OOR Stops
Annual cost of additional stops (stops × stop rate × volume)
OOR Fuel Surcharge
Annual FSC cost on out-of-route miles (FSC rate × OOR miles × volume)
Total Carrier Revenue Addition
Total Carrier Revenue
Sum of all annual revenue components — the total financial impact of this backhaul lane on your operation
Screenshot: Estimate Report — full table view
💡 Click Print / Save as PDF in the top-right of the report to export it. Use Edit Estimate Request to adjust dates, annual volume, or minimum net credit and regenerate the report at any time.
